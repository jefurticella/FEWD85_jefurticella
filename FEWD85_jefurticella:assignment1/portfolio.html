Jeffrey Furticella

Experience

Jeffrey Furticella is a Brooklyn-based sports photo editor for The New York Times. Previously he supervised the sports photo desk for The Associated Press in the company's New York headquarters, was a photo editor at Sporting News Magazine and initially was a photojournalist for the Times of Northwest Indiana. He also interned at the Patuxent Publishing Company and the Muncie Star Press and attended the Eddie Adams Workshop, Barnstorm XIX. When he is not looking for new ways to combine his love of sports and photography, he is committed to the pursuit of great pizza.

Portfolio

  • Sports Picture Editor The New York Times 2013-present

    Staff photo editor for the sports department responsible for ensuring that the visual presentation of the newspaper and Web site is creative, visually arresting and consistent with New York Times style, assigning and editing sports photographs for the newspaper and all digital platforms and creating slide shows and visual packages that complement articles or stand alone as web-only features.

  • Picture Editor Associated Press 2011-2013

    Headquarters photo editor responsible for the intake and processing of news, sports, business and entertainment photos, filed by photographers in the U.S and other parts of the world, reviewing and editing the photos for picture quality and caption accuracy, for transmission to AP Web, print and broadcast customers worldwide as well as providing timely and constructive feedback to photographers and editors in the field with regards to successful or missing coverage elements.

  • Picture Editor Sporting News Magazine 2008-2011

    Photo editor for biweekly magazine, daily digital iPad publication, and website. Responsible for editing large quantities of images for a variety of platforms, working with outside resources to acquire unique photography, coordinating with photographers to execute portrait projects, and producing slideshows.
